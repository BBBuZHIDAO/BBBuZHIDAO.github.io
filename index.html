Hekai Bu

Master’s Student in Solid Mechanics, Wuhan University
Wuhan, China

I am a master’s student in Solid Mechanics at Wuhan University. My research focuses on nanoscale heat transfer, interfacial thermal transport, and atomistic simulations of two-dimensional and van der Waals materials. I also develop and optimize scientific simulation software, with particular interests in GPU-accelerated molecular dynamics, machine-learning interatomic potentials, and hybrid force-field frameworks for large-scale materials simulations.

Research

Interfacial Thermal Transport in van der Waals Materials

I investigate phonon transport mechanisms across interfaces in two-dimensional heterostructures, using molecular dynamics simulations and hybrid potentials to understand interfacial thermal conductance in graphene, h-BN, and transition metal dichalcogenide systems.

Phonon Transport & Atomistic Simulations

I study thermal transport at the nanoscale through large-scale molecular dynamics, characterizing phonon dispersion, lifetime, and scattering mechanisms in low-dimensional materials with GPU-accelerated simulation frameworks.

Machine-Learning & Hybrid Interatomic Potentials

I develop modular frameworks that combine machine-learning potentials with classical force fields to achieve near-DFT accuracy at empirical-potential cost, and integrate these hybrid schemes into production molecular dynamics codes.
