Hekai Bu

Master’s Student in Solid Mechanics
Wuhan University · Wuhan, China

I am a master’s student in Solid Mechanics at Wuhan University. My research focuses on nanoscale heat transfer, interfacial thermal transport, and atomistic simulations of two-dimensional and van der Waals materials. I also develop and optimize scientific simulation software, with particular interests in GPU-accelerated molecular dynamics, machine-learning interatomic potentials, and hybrid force-field frameworks for large-scale materials simulations.

Research

Interfacial Thermal Transport in van der Waals Materials

I investigate phonon transport across interfaces in two-dimensional heterostructures, using molecular dynamics and hybrid potentials to understand thermal conductance in graphene, h-BN, and transition metal dichalcogenide systems.

Phonon Transport & Atomistic Simulations

I study thermal transport at the nanoscale through large-scale molecular dynamics, characterizing phonon dispersion, lifetime, and scattering mechanisms in low-dimensional materials with GPU-accelerated frameworks.

Machine-Learning & Hybrid Interatomic Potentials

I develop modular frameworks that combine machine-learning potentials with classical force fields to achieve near-DFT accuracy at empirical-potential cost, and integrate these schemes into production molecular dynamics codes.

Selected Work

Hybrid Machine-Learning & Physics-Based Potentials

Developed a hybrid sMLP+ILP framework that combines near-DFT accuracy with empirical-potential efficiency, reducing training configurations by more than one order of magnitude. Validated against experimental benchmarks for graphene, h-BN, and MoS2.

JMPS 2026 · JCTC 2026

GPUMD/NEP Development & GPU Acceleration

Profiled and optimized GPU kernels achieving ~15% speedup via algorithmic refactoring. Integrated hybrid potentials (SW+ILP, Tersoff+ILP, NEP+ILP) and developed loading-spring friction modules for GPU-accelerated dynamic simulations.

MGE Advances 2025

Interfacial Thermal Transport in 2D Heterostructures

Characterized moiré-driven thermal transport in twisted transition metal dichalcogenides and chirality-dependent strain effects on thermal conductivity in MoS2 nanotubes using large-scale MD simulations.

ACS Nano 2025 · ICHMT 2026

Development

Key contributor to GPUMD and NEP (Jul. 2023 – Present), focusing on GPU kernel optimization, hybrid potential integration, and simulation feature development.

GPU Kernel Optimization

Profiled performance bottlenecks with NVIDIA Nsight Compute and refactored critical GPU kernels, achieving approximately 15% speedup through algorithmic optimization. PR #762 PR #806

Hybrid Potential Implementation

Integrated SW+ILP, Tersoff+ILP, and NEP+ILP hybrid potentials in GPUMD and LAMMPS. Maintained the NEP_CPU repository and extended the NEP–LAMMPS interface for hybrid potential support. PR #761 PR #1062 PR #951 NEP_CPU #34

Friction Simulation Modules

Developed loading-spring force modules for GPU-accelerated dynamic friction simulations, expanding GPUMD’s capabilities for tribological modeling.

Deep Potential Interface & Documentation

Designed a ghost-atom computation module that reduces GPU memory footprint and improves throughput for the Deep Potential interface. Updated the official GPUMD user manual and internal developer documentation. PR #827 PR #1068 PR #791
